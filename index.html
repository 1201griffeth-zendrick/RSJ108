How to make an amazing monogram

Assembly time: 2 hours
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Do you need a logo to represent you as person? Perhaps you need a design that shows what you or your company are all about. This tutorial will tell you how to construct your useful and attention-grabbing monogram using some planning and Adobe products.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Paper
  • Pencil
  • A computer (preferrably a mac) with Adobe Illustrate
  • Access to friends or family

  1. Choose three words to represent your brand.

    This will be a huge help in coming up wih ideas and designs for your monogram when brainstorming.

  2. Draw ideas

    Whether you are using a design matrix or a completely blank canvas, take some time to draw ideas that represent the words you wrote in the last step. Be sure to include the letters representing your brand. 9 sketches of ideas is a good baseline.

  3. Finalize and choose a design

    Out of all the designs you made, pick the one you like the most and will fit the needs of your target audience. Don't throw away that idea sheet however, it may be useful for changes later.

  4. Research fonts and install them

    It's time to look for fonts that are similar in shape and style to your design, but if you can't find a font that doesn't suit exactly what you want you can manipulate shapes in Adobe Illustrator. The best places to look could be at www.dafont.com or www.fontspace.com. On dafont, find the font you want them hit the Download button. Once that finished upen it up in the archive utility and then open up the folder likely titled as the same font you donwloaded. After that, double click the .tff file that appears in the folder and hit the install font button.

  5. Starting the creation process

    Here the monogram is going to be made.
    Open illustrator and open a new document 800 pixels high and 800 pixels wide.
    From here click on the Type Tool.
    Find an open area on the artboard, click, then type the two letters that you are using for your brand.
    If you are not going to use a font, use the Next, open the properties panel on the right and change the font size so that it fills around 75% of the artboard.
    Change the font of the text to the font that you chose earlier.
    Right click the text and select create outlines. Click the direct selection tool.

  6. Shaping the monogram

    Drag your mouse over the pieces of the text that you would like to edit. From here it's all about messing with the anchor points to get the shape you want and making it look like the design you made earlier. The video below talks all about using the direct selection tool and how it manipulates points.

  7. Merging and cutting the shapes

    From here you're going to need to move the shapes to the positions you would like the to be in. By using the direct selection tool you should be able to move objects around and make them move. Move them to the positions your design looks like.
    From here, select the entire shape using the direct selection tool by draging a square over the shape then go up to the top of the window and select the "Object" Tab
    Scroll down and select the "Path" then "Offset Path" option that appears off of the "Path" option. Click "OK" when the dialogue box appears.
    Select the enitre shape again using the direct object tool and then select the shape builder tool on the left.
    Delete all the outlines that don't fit into your vision or shape by hovering your cursor over an area, holding the option key on the keyboard and clicking.

  8. Save your work

    When finished save your work by moving your cursor to the top of the screen and selecting "File" then "Export" then "Export as" and choose the desired file format you would like your monogram to be saved as.

  9. Take criticism and improve your work

    Be sure to talk friends and family about your monogram. Ask what they like about it, what they dislike, and what would make it stand out more.
